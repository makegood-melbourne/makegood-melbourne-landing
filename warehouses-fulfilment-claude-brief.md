# Warehouses & Fulfilment Industry Page Brief

## Purpose

Create a dedicated industry page for **warehouse and fulfilment centre make good projects in Melbourne**. The page should help commercial tenants, property managers and facility managers recognise that Make Good Melbourne handles the specific handover works common in warehouses, distribution warehouses, 3PL facilities, storage facilities, dispatch centres and e-commerce fulfilment operations.

This page should **not** read like a page for road freight operators, transport yards, heavy vehicle depots or fleet servicing businesses. The word **logistics** can be used, but it should be clearly framed as warehousing, fulfilment, storage and distribution operations.

## Voice and Copy Rules

The page should sound like a practical contractor explaining the work. It should be specific, grounded and commercial, not like generic SEO content. Use Australian English spelling and avoid an Oxford comma style.

Avoid these angles and keep the copy practical:

| Avoid | Better direction |
|---|---|
| road freight operators, truck fleets, fleet depots and vehicle servicing | fulfilment centres, warehouse tenancies, storage facilities, distribution warehouses and dispatch operations |
| generic property-investment wording | property, facility, tenancy or building |
| over-polished transition claims | coordinated, properly planned, smooth or on time |
| overdone precision adjectives | detailed, thorough or careful |
| hygiene-style wording for handover presentation | cleared, final presentation, ready for handover or site presentation |
| invented urgent-service categories | urgent handover preparation or tight handover programme |
| tenant-finance recovery language | lease handover, property manager review and handover documentation |
| broad AI-style slogans | practical statements about the exact works performed |

## Suggested Page Structure

### 1. Hero Section

The hero should quickly establish the specific property type and commercial problem. It should mention warehouses, fulfilment centres, 3PL facilities and storage sites. It should not over-explain road transport operations.

Suggested copy direction:

> Warehouse and fulfilment tenancies often leave behind racking, slab anchors, worn traffic areas, line marking, office pods, packing benches and service changes. Make Good Melbourne coordinates the strip out, repairs and handover works needed to return the property in line with the lease requirements.

The hero CTA should focus on requesting a quote or discussing the handover scope through the site form.

### 2. Intro Section

This section should explain that warehouse make good work is different from office make good because the physical wear is concentrated around racking, forklifts, pallet movement, dispatch zones, loading areas and warehouse flooring.

It should also make clear that the business can coordinate with the client’s own trades where needed, while managing the remaining make good package.

### 3. Common Warehouse Handover Works

Use a practical section with short paragraphs or cards. The likely items are:

| Work area | Copy direction |
|---|---|
| Pallet racking removal | Dismantling racking, removing fixings and preparing slab areas after anchors are taken out. |
| Slab and floor repairs | Addressing anchor holes, damaged concrete, traffic wear, floor coatings and surface preparation. |
| Line marking | Removing, refreshing or reinstating warehouse, loading bay and pedestrian line marking to suit the agreed handover scope. |
| Internal strip out | Removing packing benches, office pods, partitions, redundant fixtures and tenancy-specific fitout. |
| Make safe coordination | Coordinating electrical, plumbing, fire service, HVAC or data isolation before removal works begin. |
| Patching and painting | Repairing walls, columns, office areas and post-removal damage before final presentation. |
| End-of-lease clearing | Coordinating final site presentation, waste removal and handover preparation. |

### 6. Suggested FAQ Topics

The FAQ should be unique to warehouse and fulfilment handover. Good questions include:

| FAQ question | Answer direction |
|---|---|
| Do you remove pallet racking before warehouse handover? | Yes. Explain racking dismantling, slab anchor removal, coordination and preparation for related floor repairs. |
| Can you repair slab damage from racking anchors and forklift traffic? | Yes. Explain anchor holes, concrete repairs, grinding, patching and surface preparation where required. |
| Do you handle line marking as part of the make good? | Yes. Explain removal, reinstatement or updating line marking to match the handover scope. |
| Can you work around a staged warehouse move? | Yes. Explain staging, coordination and working around relocation timing where practical. |
| Is this service for transport depots? | Keep this careful. Say the page is primarily for warehouses, fulfilment centres and storage/distribution tenancies, while related light industrial facilities can be reviewed based on the handover scope. |
| Can you coordinate with our own trades? | Yes. Explain that Make Good Melbourne can coordinate with client-provided trades for specific items while managing the rest of the make good package. |

### 7. CTA Placement

The CTA should be the final content block after the FAQ. It should invite users to send lease documents, photos or a scope through the website form so the handover works can be reviewed.

Suggested CTA direction:

> Preparing a warehouse, fulfilment centre or storage facility for handover? Send through the lease requirements, photos or make good scope and we can review the works needed to return the tenancy for handover.
